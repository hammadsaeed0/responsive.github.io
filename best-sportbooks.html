Why do Odds vary between different Online Sportsbooks?

There are several reasons why the odds vary at different online sportsbooks. Sometimes the odds compilers at rival sportsbooks have a different opinion on how a game might unfold, so they provide distinctive odds on the outcome. In other cases, one betting site might offer more attractive odds than its rivals in a bid to drum up new business and seize market share from its competitors. The odds on a sporting event also change to reflect the amount of money wagered on either team,andsome sportsbooks are slower to react than others, presenting bettors with the opportunity to quickly gain an edge.

Is a Bookmaker the same as a Sportsbook?

A bookmaker isexactly the sameas a sportsbook. The worlds first bookmaker set up shop in the United Kingdom back in the 1790s, and British punters still use the term bookmaker to refer to an individual or establishment that takes wagers. The term sportsbook became morewidely used in the United States in order to differentiate between an establishment accepting bets on horse racing (racebook) and an establishment taking bets on all other sports (sportsbook). Yet both terms are now used across the world, and they essentially refer to the same thing.
